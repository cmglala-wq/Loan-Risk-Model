Cash-Flow Risk Assessment Model

This risk assessment model evaluates applicant creditworthiness through a cash-flow lens using 6 key criteria extracted from MyBambu's existing credit indicators. The model produces a composite risk score (0-100) where higher scores indicate lower risk, with automated decisioning thresholds for approval, manual review, and denial.

1. Transaction History

Weight: 20%

Definition

Measures the depth and stability of banking relationship through historical transaction data.

Data Points

  • Number of days of transaction history
  • Transaction pattern consistency (alignment with bank pattern)
  • Main account concentration (focus on primary account vs. scattered accounts)

Cash-Flow Risk Contribution

Transaction history serves as the foundation for all other metrics. Insufficient history (< 120 days) creates uncertainty around income stability, spending patterns, and behavioral norms. Longer history enables better prediction of future cash flow patterns and identifies seasonal variations.

Scoring Method

Risk Level Days of History Score Range Weight Factor
High Risk < 60 days 0-25 0.00x
Elevated Risk 60-119 days 26-50 0.50x
Moderate Risk 120-181 days 51-75 0.75x
Low Risk 182+ days (6+ months) 76-100 1.00x
Calculation Formula
Base Score = MIN(100, (Days of History / 182) × 100)

Adjustment Factors:
- Transaction pattern consistency: +10 if aligned with bank pattern
- Main account concentration: +10 if >80% transactions in primary account
- Data quality flag: -20 if suspected data issues

Final Score = Base Score + Adjustments (capped at 0-100)

Risk Thresholds & Decisioning

  • High Risk (0-25): Auto-deny - Insufficient data for reliable cash flow assessment
  • Elevated Risk (26-50): Requires full manual review + committee discussion if combined with ANY other moderate/elevated risk
  • Moderate Risk (51-75): Standard review process
  • Low Risk (76-100): Category-approved for this criterion

Red Flags

  • History < 120 days with multiple account changes
  • Transaction history inconsistent with stated employment
  • Suspected account switching (long periods of inactivity)

2. Parcel Affordability

Weight: 25% (HIGHEST)

Definition

Measures the applicant's ability to afford loan payments based on available cash flow after essential expenses.

Data Points

  • Minimum balance score (cushion after payments)
  • Collection percentage (disposable income ratio)
  • Secondary account affordability (additional liquidity sources)

Cash-Flow Risk Contribution

This is the MOST CRITICAL cash-flow metric. It directly measures whether the applicant has sufficient monthly cash flow to service the loan without triggering overdrafts, NSF fees, or payment defaults. Poor affordability (< 60%) indicates cash flow stress and high default probability.

Scoring Method

Risk Level Affordability % Score Range Interpretation
High Risk < 50% 0-25 Severe cash flow deficit
Elevated Risk 50.01-64.99% 26-50 Tight cash flow, high stress
Moderate Risk 65-74.99% 51-75 Adequate but limited buffer
Low Risk 75-100% 76-100 Strong cash flow cushion
Calculation Formula
Base Affordability Score = (Available Cash Flow / Required Payment) × 100

Available Cash Flow = (Monthly Income - Essential Expenses - Existing Debt Payments)

Adjustment Factors:
- Secondary account with strong affordability (>75%): +15 points
- Minimum balance consistently positive: +10 points
- Collection percentage > 80%: +5 points

Final Score = Base Score + Adjustments (capped at 0-100)

Red Flags

  • Affordability < 60% with declining income trend
  • Multiple overdrafts in recent 90 days
  • Minimum balance frequently negative

4. Employment

Weight: 15%

Definition

Assesses income stability, consistency, and employment continuity as foundation for cash flow predictability.

Data Points

  • Coefficient of variation (income stability)
  • Correlation metric (income trend direction)
  • Employment continuity (months employed / total months)
  • Recent employment (employed in last 3 months)
  • Payroll percentage (% of payroll deposited to connected accounts)

Scoring Method

Risk Level Employment Score % Score Range Key Indicators
High Risk < 25% 0-25 Not employed or severe instability
Elevated Risk 25-59.99% 26-50 Unstable income or employment gaps
Moderate Risk 60-69.99% 51-75 Moderate stability with some concerns
Low Risk 70-100% 76-100 Stable, consistent employment
Calculation Formula
Base Employment Score = 100

Deductions:
- Coefficient of variation > 0.5: -30 points (high instability)
- Coefficient of variation 0.3-0.5: -15 points (moderate instability)
- Employment ratio < 50%: -25 points
- NOT employed in last 3 months: -40 points (critical red flag)
- Payroll capture < 85%: -20 points

Bonuses:
- Employed in last 3 months: +10 points
- Employment ratio > 90%: +10 points
- Correlation trending positive: +5 points

Final Score = Base Score - Deductions + Bonuses (capped at 0-100)

Red Flags

  • Not employed in last 3 months
  • Income coefficient of variation > 0.5
  • Less than 50% of income from employment
  • Payroll capture < 70%
  • Declining income trend (negative correlation)

Definition

Evaluates financial conduct, responsibility, and red-flag behaviors that indicate elevated default risk regardless of cash flow adequacy.

Data Points

Composite Risk Score Calculation

Overall Score Formula

COMPOSITE SCORE = (Transaction History × 0.20) +
                  (Parcel Affordability × 0.25) +
                  (Debt × 0.20) +
                  (Employment × 0.15) +
                  (Behavior × 0.10) +
                  (Bank Account Stability × 0.10)

Score Range: 0-100 (where higher = lower risk)

Weight Justification

Criterion Weight Justification
Parcel Affordability 25% Direct measure of ability to afford payment; most predictive of default
Transaction History 20% Foundation metric; more history = more reliable other metrics
Debt 20% Existing obligations directly impact available cash flow
Employment 15% Income stability; less weight because temporary instability can be mitigated
Behavior 10% Powerful override metric but less common
Bank Stability 10% Indicates flight risk; lower weight because can be situational

Decision Rules & Thresholds

Automated Decisioning Framework

TIER 1: AUTO-DENY (Score 0-49.99)

Any Single "High Risk" Indicator:

  • Transaction History < 60 days
  • Parcel Affordability < 50%
  • Debt Payment History < 25%
  • Employment not in last 3 months
  • Stop payments in last 90 days
  • Gambling > 50% of income
  • 3+ payroll changes in 90 days OR 6+ in 365 days
  • Account negative >$1K for 7+ days + inactive

Specific Rule Combinations:

  • 2+ Elevated Risk in Debt category
  • All Bank Stability indicators Elevated Risk
  • Any Behavior metric = High Risk
TIER 2: MANUAL REVIEW - ELEVATED SCRUTINY (Score 50-59.99)

Elevated Risk + Compensating Factors Needed:

  • Transaction History 60-119 days (require strong other metrics)
  • Parcel Affordability 50-64.99% (require low debt + stable employment)
  • 1 Elevated + 2 Moderate in Debt
  • Employment 25-59.99% (require strong affordability + low debt)

Process: Underwriter review required, must document compensating factors, committee approval for Elevated Transaction History combinations.

TIER 3: MANUAL REVIEW - STANDARD (Score 60-69.99)

Profile Characteristics:

  • Moderate Risk or better on all scores (max 1 elevated)
  • 1 Key area Elevated + 3 Moderate (borderline case)
  • New banking relationships with otherwise strong profile

Process: Standard underwriter review, may approve with standard monitoring.

TIER 4: AUTO-APPROVE (Score 70-100)

Required Conditions (ALL must be met):

  • No High Risk indicators
  • Maximum of 1 Moderate Risk indicator
  • All others Low Risk
  • Parcel Affordability ≥ 70%
  • Transaction History ≥ 120 days

Ideal Profile - Category Approved:

  • All 6 criteria = Low Risk (76-100 on each)
  • Transaction History ≥ 182 days
  • Parcel Affordability ≥ 75%
  • Clean behavior (no derogatory marks)

Resubmission Protocol

Automated Resubmission with Account Reconnection

Trigger: User denied due to insufficient account data

  1. User auto-receives custom message: "Please connect missing bank account for re-evaluation"
  2. Upon connection:
    • All scores recalculated automatically
    • Parcel affordability recalculated across ALL accounts
    • If all scores ≥ Moderate Risk (60+) → Auto-approve

Manual Resubmission Scenarios

Scenario 1: High Risk + New Account Indicator

Conditions: High/Elevated Transaction History, clear "New Account Deposit" indicator, only 1 account connected

Action: Recalculate and potentially approve if second account demonstrates stability

Scenario 2: Elevated Transaction History + Other Strengths

Conditions: Elevated Risk Transaction History, Moderate+ on all other categories, 15% of paystub or transfers

Action: Approve with monitoring

Scenario 3: Employment Category Issues

Conditions: Employment showing elevated risk due to data categorization (income not labeled "payroll" but matches paystub ≥85%)

Action: Approve - this is a data categorization issue, not true risk

Key Insights & Pattern Analysis

Critical Findings

1. Parcel Affordability is King

  • Below 60% is the strongest predictor of default
  • 75% threshold represents the "safety zone"
  • Users with 50-65% affordability struggle even with clean credit

2. Transaction History Provides Foundation

  • Less than 120 days = unreliable data for all other metrics
  • 182-day threshold enables seasonal pattern detection

3. Debt Complexity Requires Multi-Metric Approach

  • Recent loan acceleration (+25% in 30 days) is RED FLAG
  • Multiple lenders simultaneously = 5x default rate

4. Behavioral Red Flags Override Cash Flow

  • Stop payments = intentional non-payment → Always deny
  • Gambling >50% = cash flow diversion → Always deny

Red Flag Patterns

Pattern 1: The "Desperate Borrower"

Profile: Recent loan velocity +25%, Multiple new loans (3+ in 30 days), Parcel affordability 50-65%

Interpretation: Using loans to cover living expenses, entering debt spiral

Action: DENY - high likelihood of default within 60 days

Pattern 2: The "Account Hopper"

Profile: 2+ payroll changes in 90 days, Abandoned negative account(s), Transaction gaps

Interpretation: Possible collection evasion; may be fleeing creditors

Action: DENY - enhanced verification required

Pattern 3: The "Data Quality Issue"

Profile: Elevated employment risk BUT income matches paystub ≥85%, Employed in last 3 months

Interpretation: System categorization error, not true risk

Action: APPROVE - if other metrics strong

Pattern 4: The "New Banking Relationship"

Profile: Transaction History 60-119 days, Clear "New Account Deposit", Otherwise clean metrics

Interpretation: Legitimate new banking relationship

Action: Request additional account; auto-resubmit

Pattern 5: The "Intentional Defaulter"

Profile: Stop payments in last 90 days, May have adequate income/affordability

Interpretation: Willful non-payment; character issue not capacity

Action: ALWAYS DENY - behavioral disqualification

Conclusion

This cash-flow risk assessment model provides a comprehensive, data-driven framework for credit evaluation at MyBambu. The model enables:

  • Automated decisioning for 60-70% of applications
  • Consistent, fair evaluation across all applicants
  • Clear denial reasons mapped to specific failed criteria
  • Actionable resubmission paths for borderline cases
  • Risk-calibrated approval bands with predictable default rates

Critical Success Factors

  1. Parcel Affordability drives the model (25% weight) - most predictive metric
  2. Behavioral red flags override positive cash flow - character matters
  3. Transaction history length enables reliable assessment of all other metrics
  4. Composite scoring prevents single-metric bias
  5. Hard stops for critical thresholds protect portfolio quality

The model balances access (capturing creditworthy applicants) with risk management (protecting against default), aligned with MyBambu's mission to serve the unbanked/underbanked population responsibly.
